Background

Using devices such as Jawbone Up, Nike FuelBand, and Fitbit it is now possible to collect a large amount of data about personal activity relatively inexpensively. These type of devices are part of the quantified self movement? a group of enthusiasts who take measurements about themselves regularly to improve their health, to find patterns in their behavior, or because they are tech geeks. One thing that people regularly do is quantify how much of a particular activity they do, but they rarely quantify how well they do it. In this project, your goal will be to use data from accelerometers on the belt, forearm, arm, and dumbell of 6 participants. They were asked to perform barbell lifts correctly and incorrectly in 5 different ways. More information is available from the website here: http://groupware.les.inf.puc-rio.br/har (see the section on the Weight Lifting Exercise Dataset).

The Question

The goal of your project is to predict the manner in which they did the exercise. This is the classe variable in the training set.

Analysis

I partitioned the data set into training & testing, selected designated predictors

  • Used the training data provided to partion my own training and testing sets for cross validation
  • Based on the Background, selected only the predictors that are accelerometers.
  • Created a train predictor/feature for use with the radomForest call later
inTrain <- createDataPartition(y=data_train$classe,
                               p=0.7, list = FALSE)
train <- data_train[inTrain,]
test <- data_train[-inTrain,]
train<- train %>%
  select(starts_with("accel_"), classe)
feature<- train$classe
predictors<- train %>%
  select(starts_with("accel_"))
test<- test %>%
  select(starts_with("accel_"), classe)

I used the training sub-set to train the ML algorithms using train() & randomForest() - was much faster than train for df

  • modFit1 is rpart
  • modFit2 is a rf model

Plotted the tree for modFit1 and table of importance for modFit2

modFit1 <- train(classe ~ ., method="rpart", data=train)
modFit2<-randomForest(predictors, feature)
